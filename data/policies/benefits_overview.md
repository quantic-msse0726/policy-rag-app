# Employee Benefits Overview

## Scope

This document summarizes the benefits available to eligible employees. It is an overview; specific plans, coverage, costs, and conditions are detailed in the benefits enrollment materials, summary plan descriptions (SPDs), and carrier documents. Benefits are subject to change; consult HR or the benefits portal for the latest information. This overview does not create a contractual obligation; the plan documents govern. New hires should review this during onboarding; all employees should review during open enrollment.

## Definitions

- **Eligible employee**: An employee who meets the criteria for benefits (e.g., full-time, part-time at a certain threshold).
- **Open enrollment**: The annual period when employees may enroll or change benefits.
- **Qualifying life event**: An event (e.g., marriage, birth, loss of other coverage) that allows mid-year enrollment or changes.
- **Dependent**: Spouse, domestic partner, or children as defined by the plans.

## Health Benefits

### Medical

- **Plans**: Choice of plans (e.g., PPO, HDHP with HSA). Coverage levels (employee, employee + spouse, employee + children, family) are available.
- **Employer contribution**: The company contributes a portion of premiums. Employee pays the remainder via payroll deduction.
- **Networks**: In-network and out-of-network options vary by plan. Review the provider directory.
- **Preventive care**: Covered at 100% when using in-network providers per plan terms.

### Dental

- **Coverage**: Preventive (cleanings, X-rays), basic (fillings, extractions), and major (crowns, root canals) services.
- **Annual maximum**: Plan-specific. Orthodontia may be available for dependents.

### Vision

- **Coverage**: Annual eye exam and allowance for glasses or contacts. Frame and lens options vary.

### Life and AD&D

- **Basic life**: 1x annual salary, company-paid. Beneficiary designation required.
- **Supplemental life**: Optional employee-paid coverage for self and dependents. Subject to evidence of insurability above certain amounts.

### Short- and Long-Term Disability

- **STD**: Income replacement for qualifying short-term absences (e.g., illness, injury). Benefit level and waiting period vary by plan.
- **LTD**: Income replacement for qualifying long-term disability. Benefit level, waiting period, and definition of disability vary by plan.

## Retirement

### 401(k) Plan

- **Employee contributions**: Up to IRS limits. Elect via payroll deduction.
- **Employer match**: Up to 4% of eligible compensation, subject to plan terms. Match may have a vesting schedule.
- **Vesting**: Employer contributions typically vest over 4 years (25% per year) or per plan document.
- **Investments**: Choose from plan-approved investment options. Default option available for those who do not elect.

## Flexible Spending

### Healthcare FSA

- Pre-tax contributions for eligible healthcare expenses.
- Use-it-or-lose-it rule applies per plan; some plans offer carryover or grace period.
- Maximum contribution is set annually by IRS.

### Dependent Care FSA

- Pre-tax contributions for eligible dependent care (e.g., daycare, after-school care).
- Use-it-or-lose-it rule applies.
- Maximum contribution is set annually by IRS.

### HSA (High Deductible Health Plan Participants)

- Tax-advantaged savings for healthcare expenses.
- Employer may make contributions.
- Funds roll over; no use-it-or-lose-it.
- Eligibility and contribution limits are set by IRS.

## Other Benefits

### Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

- Confidential counseling and support for mental health, financial, legal, and other personal concerns.
- No cost to employees. Contact information is provided in benefits materials.

### Commuter Benefits

- Pre-tax transit and parking where available. Enrollment is separate from medical benefits.

### Wellness Programs

- Gym reimbursement, wellness challenges, and mental health resources may be available.
- Participation is voluntary. Incentives may apply per program terms.

### Professional Development

- Stipend or reimbursement for courses, conferences, and certifications. See the **Expenses Policy** and internal guidelines for approval and limits.

## Eligibility

- **Full-time employees**: Eligible from first day or first of month following hire, per plan terms.
- **Part-time employees**: May be eligible for prorated or limited benefits based on hours. Consult HR.
- **Dependents**: Coverage for spouse, domestic partner, and children per plan definitions.
- **New hires**: Enroll within 30 days of eligibility. Defaults may apply if no election is made.

## Enrollment

- **New hires**: Enroll within 30 days of eligibility. Elections are generally effective from the first day of eligibility.
- **Open enrollment**: Typically annual. Changes take effect the following plan year unless a qualifying life event occurs.
- **Qualifying life events**: Marriage, birth, adoption, loss of other coverage, etc. Documentation required. Changes must be requested within the plan's deadline (often 30 days).

## Procedures

### Enrolling or Making Changes

1. Log into the benefits portal during open enrollment or within 30 days of a qualifying life event.
2. Select or update plans and dependents.
3. Review costs and confirm. Elections are binding for the plan year unless a qualifying life event occurs.
4. Complete any required forms (e.g., HSA, beneficiary designation).

## FAQ

**Q: When do my benefits start?**  
A: Typically the first day of employment or the first of the month following hire. Check your offer letter and benefits materials.

**Q: Can I add my domestic partner?**  
A: Yes, if the plan allows. You may need to complete an affidavit of domestic partnership. Consult HR.

**Q: What if I miss open enrollment?**  
A: You may be defaulted to existing elections or no coverage, depending on the plan. Mid-year changes require a qualifying life event.

**Q: Is the EAP confidential?**  
A: Yes. Use of the EAP is confidential within legal limits. The company does not receive details of your sessions.
