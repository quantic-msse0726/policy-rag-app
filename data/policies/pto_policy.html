Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

Scope

This policy establishes guidelines for paid time off for full-time and eligible part-time employees. PTO supports work-life balance while maintaining business continuity.

Definitions

  • PTO: Bank of days for vacation, personal time, or short-term illness.
  • Immediate family: Spouse, domestic partner, children, parents, siblings, grandparents, grandchildren, step-family.

Eligibility

  • Full-time employees accrue from first day of employment.
  • Part-time (20+ hrs/week): prorated PTO.
  • Contractors and temporary workers are not eligible.

Accrual Rates

Years of ServiceAnnual Accrual
0–2 years15 days
3–5 years20 days
6+ years25 days

Carryover: up to 5 days. Excess forfeited January 31.

Policy

Requesting Time Off

  1. Submit via HR portal at least 14 days in advance when possible.
  2. Emergency requests: notify manager immediately; log within 48 hours.
  3. Manager approval required. Conflicts resolved by submission seniority.

Special Provisions

  • Bereavement: Up to 5 days for immediate family.
  • Jury duty: Paid leave for duration.
  • Parental leave: 12 weeks (primary caregiver), 2 weeks (secondary).
  • Voting: Up to 2 hours paid when needed.

Enforcement

Abuse may result in disciplinary action. Managers must process requests promptly.

FAQ

Half-day increments? Yes, with manager approval.

PTO during disability? Does not accrue; existing balance may supplement.
