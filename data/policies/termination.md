# Termination and Offboarding Policy

## Scope

This policy covers voluntary resignations, involuntary terminations, and the offboarding process for all employees. It ensures consistent, respectful treatment and protection of company and employee interests. This policy applies to all employees; contractors may have different terms per their agreement. The policy works in conjunction with the **Equipment and Asset Management Policy** (equipment return) and **Information Security Policy** (access revocation). HR owns this policy; managers and IT support execution.

## Definitions

- **Voluntary resignation**: Employee-initiated separation (e.g., resignation, retirement).
- **Involuntary termination**: Company-initiated separation (e.g., for cause, redundancy, restructuring).
- **Notice period**: The period between notice and the last day of employment.
- **Offboarding**: The process of transitioning an employee out of the organization, including access revocation, equipment return, and knowledge transfer.

## Voluntary Resignation

- **Notice**: Employees should provide written notice as specified in their offer letter (typically 2 weeks for non-exempt, 2â€“4 weeks for exempt). Notice may be given via email to the manager and HR.
- **During notice**: Employees are expected to complete handover tasks, document knowledge, and assist in transition. Performance expectations remain in effect.
- **PTO**: Unused PTO is handled per the PTO Policy and applicable state law. PTO may not be used to extend the notice period.
- **Exit interview**: HR offers an exit interview to gather feedback. Participation is voluntary but encouraged.
- **Final pay**: Final paycheck and any owed amounts are processed per state law (e.g., within a defined period after separation). Benefits continuation (e.g., COBRA) information is provided.

## Involuntary Termination

- **For cause**: Terminations for performance, conduct, or policy violations. Require documentation, PIP (if applicable), and HR review. Employee is informed privately and given information on final pay, benefits, and next steps.
- **Business reasons**: Terminations due to redundancy, restructuring, or elimination of role. Notice or pay in lieu is provided per applicable law (e.g., WARN) and company policy. Severance may be offered; terms are in a separation agreement.
- **Process**: HR and the manager coordinate. The employee is informed in a private meeting. A written confirmation may be provided. Access is revoked promptly.

## Offboarding Checklist

### Employee Responsibilities

- Return all company property: laptop, badge, keys, equipment, access cards. See **Equipment and Asset Management Policy**.
- Complete handover of projects, documentation, and credentials.
- Participate in knowledge transfer as requested.
- Maintain confidentiality of company information post-employment.

### Company Responsibilities

- **Access revocation**: Revoke system access within 24 hours of separation. IT executes per HR notification.
- **Equipment**: Collect all equipment. Provide prepaid shipping labels for remote employees if applicable.
- **Final pay**: Process final paycheck and any owed amounts per state law.
- **Benefits**: Provide COBRA and benefits continuation information where applicable.
- **References**: Provide job title and dates of employment per policy. Additional reference checks require HR coordination.

### Confidentiality

- Confidentiality obligations survive termination. Employees must not disclose or use confidential information after separation. See **Confidentiality and NDA Policy**.
- Return or destroy all confidential materials. Do not retain copies.

## Procedures

### Resignation Process

1. Employee provides written notice to manager and HR.
2. Manager and HR acknowledge and confirm last day.
3. Manager assigns handover tasks and designates backup.
4. Employee completes handover and transition.
5. On last day: return equipment, complete exit checklist if provided, attend exit meeting if scheduled.
6. HR processes final pay and benefits paperwork.

### Termination Process (Involuntary)

1. HR and manager confirm decision and documentation.
2. Meeting is scheduled. HR and/or manager conducts the meeting.
3. Employee is informed of the decision, effective date, and next steps.
4. Access is revoked. Equipment is collected or return arrangements made.
5. HR processes final pay, benefits, and any separation agreement.
6. Manager communicates transition to the team as appropriate (without disparaging the former employee).

### Manager Responsibilities

- Handle separations professionally and respectfully.
- Do not discuss the separation inappropriately with other employees.
- Refer reference requests to HR. Do not provide references on company letterhead or as a formal reference without HR approval.

## Rehire Eligibility

- **Voluntary resignation in good standing**: May be eligible for rehire. HR assesses on a case-by-case basis.
- **Termination for cause**: Generally not eligible for rehire.
- **Business-related separation**: May be eligible depending on circumstances.

## Enforcement

- Employees who fail to return equipment or violate confidentiality may be liable for costs or legal action.
- Managers who handle terminations improperly may be subject to review.

## Examples

**Example 1**: An employee resigns with 2 weeks notice. They complete handover, return their laptop and badge on the last day, and participate in an exit interview. Final pay is processed within the state-required timeframe.

**Example 2**: An employee is terminated for cause following a PIP. They are informed in a meeting with HR and their manager. Access is revoked the same day. Equipment return is arranged. Final pay is processed per law.

**Example 3**: A role is eliminated in a restructuring. The employee receives notice (or pay in lieu), severance per the separation agreement, and COBRA information. They return equipment and complete offboarding.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use PTO during my notice period?**  
A: PTO use during notice is at manager discretion and must not extend the notice period. Unused PTO may be paid out per state law.

**Q: Will I get a reference?**  
A: The company provides job title and dates of employment. Detailed references require HR coordination and may be subject to company policy.

**Q: What if I disagree with the termination?**  
A: You may raise concerns with HR. The company will review. Some terminations may be subject to dispute resolution processes per your agreement or policy.
